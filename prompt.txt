**User**: Could lights or Starlink accidentally get stuck on and drain the battery? How do we troubleshoot?

**Claude**: Yes — if a scheduler crashes while a relay is on, `pinctrl` keeps the state and the relay stays on. Pi reboots are safe (GPIO resets to LOW = relays OFF), but app crashes and killed tmux sessions are dangerous.

Built three layers of protection:
1. **State files** in `power.py` — every relay toggle writes/clears a timestamp file (`.lights_on_since`, `.starlink_on_since`) in `~/salmoncv/data/`
2. **Watchdog** (`salmoncv-watchdog`) — checks state files and forces relays off if they exceed max duration. Lights max = night duration + 1h buffer (calculated from civil twilight). Starlink max = 3 hours. Logs forced shutoffs to `~/salmoncv/data/watchdog_log.csv`.
3. **Cron job** — run watchdog every 15 minutes automatically:
   `*/15 * * * * /home/nalaquq/salmoncv/venv/bin/salmoncv-watchdog`
